Ada Lovelace

The World's First Computer Programmer

Timeline of an Extraordinary Mind:

  • 1815 - Born in London to the famous poet Lord Byron and Lady Byron.
  • 1833 - Meets mathematician Charles Babbage through their mutual friend Mary Somerville, sparking a lifelong collaboration.
  • 1842 - Translates Italian mathematician Luigi Menabrea’s memoir on Babbage’s proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine.
  • 1843 - Publishes her extensive set of notes on the engine. "Note G" contains an algorithm to compute Bernoulli numbers, now recognized as the world's first complete computer program.
  • 1852 - Passes away at the young age of 36, leaving behind a legacy that wouldn't be fully appreciated until the birth of modern computing a century later.

"The Analytical Engine weaves algebraic patterns just as the Jacquard loom weaves flowers and leaves."

— Ada Lovelace
